Armenian Alphabet information text:

The Armenian Alphabet was created in the 5th century by famous scholar and translator Mesrop Mashtots (404-406).The alphabet is composed as a prayer, beginning with A as Astvats (=God) and ending with K' as K'ristos (=Christ).The original alphabet had only 36 letters. Later, three more characters were added: In the Middle Ages, two new letters (օ [o], ֆ [f]) were introduced in order to better represent foreign sounds.

Letter - և (yev) : actually a conjunction meaning "and". It is used only in minuscule.
